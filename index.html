Declarer leaderboard — four facets, 2025 World Championships

Every declarer with ≥40 boards in each division, ranked by F4 — trick-taking scored in IMPs against the field in the identical contract on the same deal. The lighter facets show the same skill through different lenses; ranking uses the stakes-weighted one.

Source Herning 2025 · RR + KO · peers = other tables in the same strain+level+side on that deal · leave-one-out

F1 · tricks vs DD

Actual tricks − double-dummy. The single-reference measure — useful, but DD is one fixed yardstick, not the field.

F2 · tricks vs field

Actual tricks − average taken by other declarers in the same contract on the deal. Pure card-play in tricks.

F3 · making vs field

Did you make it − the field's make-rate for that contract on the deal. Positive = brings home contracts peers fail.

F4 · IMPs vs field

The ranking. F2's trick edge scored in IMPs — a trick that swings a game counts for what it's worth. Field-relative and stakes-aware.

Worked example — every board Liam Milne declared (Open), all four facets

Milne declared 55 boards; 53 had at least one other table in the identical contract (needed for F2–F4). Each facet in his leaderboard row is the per-board average of the column below — nothing modelled, every board scored from the actual result and the field on that exact deal.

F1 = tricks − DD  ·  F2 = tricks − mean(peer tricks)  ·  F3 = made? − field make-rate  ·  F4 = IMP( your score − mean(peers' tricks re-scored in your contract) )

One board, all four facets — RR-r14 board 25, 4♥ by Milne (N), non-vul

Milne took 10; double-dummy says 9. Ten other tables also played 4♥ on this deal: their tricks were 10,10,10,9,9,9,9,9,9,7 (mean 9.1), three of them made it.
F1 = 10 − 9 = +1   (tricks over DD)
F2 = 10 − 9.1 = +0.90   (tricks over the same-contract field)
F3 = made(1) − field-made(3/10 = 0.30) = +0.70   (brought home one the field made 30% of the time)
F4: your score 4♥+1 = +420; re-score each peer's tricks in 4♥ non-vul (10→420, 9→−50, 7→−150) → mean +81; F4 = IMP(420 − 81) = +8   (the extra trick swung a game — worth real IMPs)

Reconciliation — every figure matches Milne's row in the Open table.
F1: Σ −1 ÷ 55 = −0.02   F2: Σ -1.7 ÷ 53 = −0.03
F3: Σ 1.6 ÷ 53 = +0.03   F4: Σ +41 IMP ÷ 53 = +0.77
F1/F2 say Milne is a dead-average declarer by trick count; F4 (+0.77, #11 in the Open) shows his tricks land on the boards that matter.

Why four. F1 leans on double-dummy; F2–F4 lean on the field, which is the fairer bar. F2/F3 are in natural units (tricks, make-rate) and ignore stakes; F4 restores stakes by scoring the same-contract trick difference in IMPs. A player can be flat on F1/F2 yet high on F4 if their tricks land on the boards that matter — e.g. Milne (Open #11, F4 +0.77) is trick-neutral but clutch, while Coutts (#107, −0.44) is the reverse. Declaring only; defence and bidding are separate. Everything is vs the full division field, so a positive number means better than a typical table at the championship.
