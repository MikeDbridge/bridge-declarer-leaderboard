Declarer leaderboard, 2019 World Championships

Every declarer with ≥40 boards in each division, ranked by the IMPs each gained versus the field in the identical contract on the same deal. The other columns show the same skill through different lenses; the ranking uses the stakes-weighted one.

Source Wuhan 2019 · RR + KO · peers = other tables in the same strain+level+side on that deal · leave-one-out

tricks v DD

Actual tricks minus double-dummy. The single-reference measure: useful, but DD is one fixed yardstick, not the field.

tricks v field

Actual tricks minus the average taken by other declarers in the same contract on the deal. Pure card-play in tricks.

make v field

Did you make it, minus the field's make-rate for that contract on the deal. Positive = brings home contracts peers fail.

IMP v field

The ranking. The trick edge vs the field scored in IMPs; a trick that swings a game counts for what it's worth. Field-relative and stakes-aware.

Peer matching

Default compares only tables where the same seat declared (2♥-E vs 2♥-E): same hand concealed, same opening leader. Toggle to either seat (the looser side match) to see the difference. Every column is averaged over the boards that had such a peer.

Reading the columns. The double-dummy column leans on one fixed yardstick; the field columns compare you to the tables that actually played your contract, which is the fairer bar. Tricks and make-rate ignore stakes; the IMP column restores them by scoring the same-contract trick difference in IMPs, so a player can be flat on tricks yet high on IMPs when their edge lands on the boards that matter. Declaring only; defence and bidding are separate. Everything is vs the full division field, so a positive number means better than a typical table at the championship.
